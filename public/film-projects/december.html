Lizette — DECEMBER

A performance-driven music video for Lizette’s debut single, exploring emotional limbo, intimacy without commitment, and the quiet violence of being half-chosen.

About

DECEMBER is a performance-driven music video created for Lizette’s debut single. Designed as a dialogue-free visual narrative, the piece treats emotional restraint as its primary storytelling device — allowing gesture, framing, and silence to carry meaning rather than exposition.

Set within a winter landscape, the visual world mirrors the emotional state of the song: closeness without clarity, warmth interrupted by absence, and the slow recognition that staying can hurt more than leaving. The video exists in the in-between — where connection feels real, but commitment never arrives.

Concept & Casting

From its inception, the music video for DECEMBER was conceived as a purely visual work — absent of dialogue and conventional narrative structure. Emotion is communicated entirely through performance, spatial relationships, and pacing.

Following an open casting process, Meekel T Riney-Kim was cast as the male lead. His grounded, understated presence became central to the video’s emotional tension, anchoring the quiet push and pull that defines the relationship onscreen.

Collaboration

Lizette first reached out in early fall with the initial idea for DECEMBER. After meeting in person during a separate creative shoot, the project quickly evolved into a collaborative process rooted in trust, vulnerability, and shared emotional clarity.

Rather than relying on a rigid shot list, the production remained intentionally flexible — allowing weather, environment, and performance to shape the final visual language.

Storyboard

The music video for DECEMBER was structured as an emotional timeline aligned with the song’s progression, rather than a traditional narrative arc.

The storyboard emphasized recurring visual motifs — isolation, proximity, and rupture — moving between night exteriors, intimate interior moments, and daylight distance. Key sequences were designed to escalate from stillness into motion before returning to restraint.

The shoot itinerary followed natural winter light: interior apartment scenes, downtown B-roll and slow motion, dockside sequences at sunset, and isolated night exteriors to close the day.

Artist & Director Interview

An excerpt from a filmed conversation between Lizette and director PJ Kim, recorded during production.

Lizette: It’s about the ache of being half-chosen by someone you’d choose every day.

It’s about being caught between closeness and distance with someone who says they care about you, but doesn’t make the effort to prove it — in other words, a situationship.

PJ: What inspired you to write DECEMBER?

Lizette: I wrote this song in December of 2022 during a late night where I was missing someone I wasn’t even sure missed me back.

I wanted to capture the confusion of feeling wanted one moment and invisible the next — set within an emotional winter where you realize you have to let go, even though every part of you still wants them.

DECEMBER is about begging yourself — and this person — to let the connection die because it’s hurting more than it’s healing.

PJ: What was the hardest part?

Lizette: Being vulnerable enough to share it. Learning to trust other creatives and collaborate was what ultimately made this project possible.

Visual Language

  • winter palette with muted tones and restrained contrast
  • performance prioritized over narrative exposition
  • negative space used as an emotional device
  • minimal camera movement to preserve intimacy

Behind the Scenes

Shot over a winter weekend in the snow, the production remained intentionally small to preserve focus and emotional proximity. The cold environment became an active component of the performance, shaping movement, pacing, and stillness.

Release

DECEMBER, Lizette’s debut single, premiered on December 6th alongside its accompanying music video across Instagram and YouTube which released on December 14th.

Director’s Note

My goal as a director is to create visual spaces where artists can express stories that are often felt but rarely articulated.

With DECEMBER, the intention was to support Lizette’s voice — translating the emotional core of her debut single into a visual language that honors restraint, vulnerability, and honesty.
